How the Music of 1991 Advanced the Music Industry

The success of the music released in the year of 1991 spanned all genres. When we think of 90s music, we typically think right to rock n roll and grunge.
However, music from all genres were released this year, each finding great success. Albums released within this one year have stood the test of time due
to their originality and boldness after the hairbands took over the 80s. The music of the 90s has a distinct sound that differs greatly from decades prior.
Artists of the following decades often cite albums from 1991 serving as inspiration for their own art. Grunge music is often credited with singlehandedly
destroying the hairbands that took over the 80s. The majority of the highly successful albums released within this year are grunge, therefore allowing the music
industry to advance. Grunge music created a completely new sound for this era by combining elements of rock, punk, and heavy metal, to create a cravable
sound music lovers still enjoy today.


The band Pearl Jam found their success after releasing their debut album "Ten" in 1991, highlighting their own interpretation of grunge music with introspective
lyrics and dark themes featured in their songs. Following the release of "Ten", Pearl Jam's fame only continued to sky rocket with their televised session on "MTV Unplugged",
and tour the following year. Currently, Pearl Jam remains one of the most popular, inspirational to other artisits, relevant, and highest grossing bands worldwide.

The band Nirvana continues to be known as one of the most influential bands from America. The drummer for Nirvana, Dave Grohl, continued his musical career after the group's
disbandment in 1994. The music Grohl took part in while he was in Nirvana would later go on to influence the music in his solo projects as the Foo Fighters.


The genres hip-hop and rap also grew in popularity during this year. Artists such as Public Enemy, A Tribe Called Quest, Ice-T, Ice Cube, and Cypress Hill
gained traction during this year with their reinvention of genres and songs about bringing light to current issues in the world. Public Enemy's "Apocalypse '91"
is considered to be a staple in any hip-hop collection by fans and critics all over. After leaving his group N.W.A. in 1989, rapper Ice Cube began his solo career and
released his highly successful solo project "Death Certificate" in 1991. This album dealt with difficult subject matter, but inspired following artists to embrace being
honest when sharing their feelings on touchy subjects and to not pull punches when saying what needs said. This is evident in artists that followed him
such as, Kendrick Lamar and Killer Mike.

Simply put, 1991 was a great year for music. Many of the albums released over it's 365 day span have stood the test of time and remain relevant today.
Sports games still use music from Metallica's "Metallica" to hype up the crowds and players before games, and bands such as the Red Hot Chili Peppers
continue to release music and tour even today, 30+ years after their debut. The music of 1991 debuted, as well as solidified, the star status of many artists and
bands. The music released in the year 1991 will continue on to influence artists and bring joy to music lovers worldwide.
